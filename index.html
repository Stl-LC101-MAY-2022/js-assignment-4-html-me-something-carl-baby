A Little About Littlejohn

Hi, I'm Carli! I'm 26 years old and I love to sightsee!
Being raised as a military kid, I learned early on that living in other places can be both exciting and challenging.
My immediate family's roots spread from California to Hawai'i, and now they've settled in the Midwest.
I've visited so many great places with my fiance and we will continue to explore as we age.
Check out a few of our trips:

  • Tulsa, Oklahoma
  • Ontario, Canada
  • Chicago, Illinois
  • Orlando, Florida
  • Oahu, Hawai'i




Tulsa, Oklahoma

At first, you may be thinking "Okay... Tulsa? and for what?"
Tulsa is a hotspot in the hardcore music community; I like to think of the genre "hardcore" nestled between metal and punk.
My fiance and I have taken many roadtrips to support our friends in bands and it's a great excuse to explore more of the country!
Tulsa has some of the kindest people in this music community, and they are incredibly talented.
If you find yourself in Tulsa, you should check out the following places:

  • Chimera: A delicious cafe specializing in incredible food and lattes AND it has an event space!
  • Foolish Things: A multi-roaster that offers our local Blueprint Coffee. It's also part art gallery, part event space.
  • Cirque Coffee: A funky coffee roaster with specialty coffee focus and now a trendy new drive-thru location.



Ontario, Canada

Ahh, Canada. Fun fact, you only need a birth certificate and driver's license to cross the border! Once you cross the border, it's country driving for hours until you hit the cities.
In Ontario, I've traveled through Toronto, Niagara Falls, and a beautiful beach town called Cobourg Beach.
I have been up here several times, and each time I feel so far from the states. However, if you ever want a short weekend trip, Niagara is only 12 hours away!
If you find yourself in Ontario, you should check out the following places:

  • Niagara Falls!!!!! I cried here from the power of almost 80,000 gallons of water moving per SECOND. Do the Canadian-side excursions.
  • Cobourg Beach: This little town is a great stopping point between Toronto and Montreal, and a perfect way to break up the drive.
  • Pilot Coffee Roasters: Now, there's a LOT of coffee in Toronto, so don't limit yourself- I just loved the aesthetic here.



Chicago, Illinois

Chicago... my love. If I didn't live in Saint Louis, I would love to move to Chicago. I'm an Illinois girl at heart. Sorry Missouri, you've got some issues to work out.
There is SO much art, food, music, and culture in Chicago. Every trip is a different experience.
I like to think of this city as the "we're official" status update when couples start dating and taking trips together.
If you find yourself in Chicago, you should check out the following places:

  • Duck Duck Goat: Good God. This restaurant blew my mind last year. It is continuously in the top 10 for best restaurants in the city. Please try it if you like inspired Chinese-American fusion cuisine.
  • The Art Institute: Brilliant design, amazing location, and enough art to keep you busy for a week. I'm very emotionally attached to this place. It's giving a main character moment.
  • DayGlow Coffee (or Intelligentsia Coffee): Both cafes are cream of the crop in the city for consistent, elevated coffee experiences. Dayglow is based out of LA and features coffee from Europe.



Orlando, Florida

If you're coming to Orlando, it's either for amusement parks... or you have family here.
I have successfully gotten my fiance to fall in love with Universal Studios and DisneyWorld. We are the non-cringy/goth form of the Disney Adult due to my family taking us several times.
Florida, overall, is a hard pass for me, but Orlando is a safe little oasis in the desert.
If you find yourself in Orlando, you should check out the following places:

  • DisneyWorld- duh! If you go during slower seasons, it's amazing. Going in with child-like amazement and allowing yourself to laugh and skip around heals your inner child.
  • Pop Century Resort: This is the most affordable resort on the Disney property, but they don't cut corners. It's colorful, retro, and has access to the new Skyliner transit.
  • Universal Studios: Now, this park is a hot mess. People are messy, grumpy, and impatient. But the new Velocicoaster is INSANE and worth it all. Just be sure to visit the Wizarding World of Harry Potter as well.



Oahu, Hawai'i

Oahu is Hawaii's most populated island and my family's home island! Though I am white, I have Native Hawaiian and Chinese ancestors on my mother's side. I like to honor them by educating people on how the United States stole this land.
You must embrace the culture on the island and be respective to all native plants and endangered animals. No one likes tourist behavior! Nothing is more disrespectful than getting in a sea turtle's personal space, and people can go to jail for it.
There are amazing people, places, and blended cultures collaborating in food and art as well. I encourage you to explore Hawai'i differently than you would Montana or Tennessee.
If you find yourself on Oahu, you should check out the following places:

  • Kualoa Ranch: My favorite tourist attraction! This active farming ranch is also an active filming sight for the Jurassic Park franchise. Kualoa now farms and sells it's own island-grown meat and produce such as chocolate, beef, and breadfruit hummus.
  • Lanikai Juice: My family lives in Kailuatown, so almost every day I get a smoothie bowl after a long day of bodysurfing and sunbathing.
  • Tane Vegan Iyazaki: If local, fresh fish isn't your thing (it's okay) this entirely vegan sushi bar is brilliant and full of flavor! You won't feel like you're missing out at all.



This concludes your tour of some of my favorite places to visit.
Remember- "Adventure is out there!"
I hope you enjoyed your stay! 😀
